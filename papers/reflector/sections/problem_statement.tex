Modern AI-augmented development systems operate in a fundamentally different regime
than the human-driven workflows for which existing software engineering methodologies
were designed. The core tension is this: the efficiency of autonomous agents scales
with their degree of autonomy, but the alignment risk scales with it as well.

\subsection{Research Questions}

This paper addresses the following research questions:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[RQ1] What structural properties of recursive AI-augmented development systems
    lead to recursive drift?
  \item[RQ2] What governance mechanisms are sufficient to prevent or detect drift while
    preserving the efficiency benefits of autonomous operation?
  \item[RQ3] How can milestone-based synchronization be implemented in a way that is
    both human-meaningful and machine-enforceable?
  \item[RQ4] What architectural patterns support the integration of reflective governance
    into existing development pipelines?
\end{enumerate}
